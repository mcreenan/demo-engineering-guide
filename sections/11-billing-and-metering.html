Every org is on one of three plans — free, team, or enterprise — each with a flat monthly price and a quota of included units. A unit is one metered action; usage above the quota is billed as overage on the next invoice, and customers are never cut off mid-cycle. This section is the canonical home for plans, invoices, overage math, and metering.

In shortThree plans (free / team / enterprise); money is integer cents, never floats; a unit is one metered action; usage events are append-only and aggregated per org per period; an invoice goes draft→open→paid (or void); the bill is base price plus overage; billing reads usage totals through the gateway, never the usage schema directly.

Units & metering

platform/services/usage/index.ts platform/services/usage/migrations/001_init.sql Usage & limits

A unit is one metered action. The UsageService records one row per action in usage.usage_events, an append-only event log — rows are only ever inserted, never updated or deleted.

record(orgId, units)
Appends an event: INSERT INTO usage_events (org_id, units, occurred_at) VALUES ($1, $2, now()). Other services record usage by calling the gateway, which routes to usage — never by calling usage directly.
totalForPeriod(orgId, start, end)
Sums units in a half-open window: SELECT COALESCE(SUM(units), 0) FROM usage_events WHERE org_id = $1 AND occurred_at >= start AND occurred_at < end. This is what billing reads when finalizing an invoice.
  • Aggregated per org per period. The index usage_events_by_org_time ON usage.usage_events (org_id, occurred_at) serves exactly this summing pattern.
  • Live count. Customers see a continuously updating usage figure on the dashboard at Dashboard → Usage.
  • Resets each cycle. Usage resets at the start of each billing cycle.
  • Never cut off. Customers are not cut off mid-cycle; usage above the included quota is billed as overage on the next invoice.
Billing reads usage through the gatewayBilling and usage are separate services owning separate schemas, so billing never queries the usage schema directly. It asks the usage service for a period total over HTTP through the gateway when finalizing an invoice — the schema-per-service rule in Platform Services and The Gateway & Request Flow. Column-level usage schema is owned by Core Domain & Data Model.

An invoice belongs to an org and is generated per organization at the end of each billing cycle. It moves through a small four-state machine: from draft, to finalized and sent, to charged — or cancelled.

  • Amount. The invoice amount is the plan base price plus any overage above the included quota, stored in amount_cents.
  • Lookups. Invoices are read with invoicesForOrg — SELECT * FROM invoices WHERE org_id = $1 ORDER BY period_start DESC, served by the index invoices_by_org ON billing.invoices (org_id, period_start DESC).

The TypeScript symbols are the Invoice interface and the BillingService methods invoicesForOrg, finalizeInvoice, and chargeInvoice.

"overdue" is not a statusThe Help Center says a long-unpaid invoice is "marked overdue" after retries fail, but overdue is not a value in the status enum — the only statuses are draft, open, paid, and void. Treat "overdue" as Help Center phrasing, not a database state.

Finalizing an invoice: base + overage

platform/services/billing/index.ts Billing & plans

finalizeInvoice closes out the current draft invoice for an org. Its documented steps:

  1. Load the plan. Read the org's plan (a join of plans and orgs) to get monthlyPriceCents and includedUnits.
  2. Get the usage total. Fetch the period total via GET /usage/total?period=current through the gateway.
  3. Compute overage. overage = max(0, units - includedUnits) * unit price.
  4. Compute the amount. amount = monthlyPriceCents + overage.
  5. Finalize. UPDATE invoices SET amount_cents, status = 'open', moving the invoice draft→open and enqueuing an invoice_receipt notification.

The per-unit overage rate is not defined in the sourcesStep 3 multiplies excess units by a unit price (UNIT_PRICE_CENTS in the code), but that constant is never assigned a value anywhere in the available sources. State the formula only — overage = max(0, units - includedUnits) * unit price — and do not assume a number for the per-unit rate.

Charging & retries

platform/services/billing/index.ts Billing & plans

chargeInvoice attempts payment on an open invoice. On success it moves the invoice open→paid; a failed charge retries for 7 days.

  • Success. The invoice transitions to paid.
  • Failure. Failed charges retry for 7 days. The Help Center then describes the invoice as "overdue" — phrasing only, not a status (see the callout above). What happens after the retry window (suspension, etc.) is not specified in the available sources.

The charge "calls the payment processor"; the specific processor or vendor is not named in the sources, though infra references a billing-secrets/payments-api-key secret — secret handling is owned by Security & Secrets.

Changing plans & usage alerts

Billing & plans Usage & limits

Customers manage their plan and payment card themselves; usage alerts are an opt-in email that the always-sent emails are not.

Upgrade / downgrade
Change plan any time at Settings → Billing. Upgrades take effect immediately; downgrades take effect at the start of the next cycle.
Payment card
Updated at Settings → Billing.
Usage alerts (opt-in)
Owners and admins can turn on a usage alert at Settings → Notifications to get an email when usage crosses a threshold (the Help Center gives 80% of quota only as an example). This is optional, unlike invoice receipts, member invites, and payment-failed emails, which are always sent — see Notifications & Webhooks. Roles are defined in Authentication & Authorization.
Reconciliation
Invoices are reconciled against metered usage; if an invoice looks wrong, contact support.
Upgrading often beats overageIf an org regularly exceeds its quota, the Help Center guidance is that upgrading to a larger plan is usually cheaper than paying overage on every invoice.
